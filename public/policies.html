Renewals

Items may be renewed in person, by phone, or online through your member dashboard — provided no other member has reserved the item. Overdue items cannot be renewed until fines are settled.

Lost or Damaged Items

Members are responsible for all borrowed materials. Lost or irreparably damaged items must be replaced with an identical or equivalent copy, or the current market value plus a LKR 250 processing fee must be paid.

Reservations (Holds)

Members may place holds on items currently on loan. Up to 3 active holds are permitted per member. Reserved items are held for 5 business days after notification before being returned to the shelf.

Membership Policy

Eligibility

Membership is open to all residents of Sri Lanka. Proof of identity (National Identity Card, Passport, or equivalent) is required at registration.

Membership Renewal

Annual memberships expire on the anniversary of registration. Members are notified by email 30 days before expiry. Memberships may be renewed online through the member portal or in person at the library.

Suspension & Cancellation

The library reserves the right to suspend or cancel membership for non-payment of fines exceeding LKR 500, repeated violation of borrowing rules, or misconduct on library premises. Suspended members may appeal to the Head Librarian within 30 days.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 January 2025

Information We Collect

  • Registration data: Full name, email, phone, address, date of birth, NIC, membership category.
  • Loan & reservation records: Which materials you borrow, when, and for how long.
  • System usage data: Login timestamps, IP addresses for security auditing.
  • AI Chat transcripts: Messages sent to the Library Assistant chat widget are stored to improve service quality and are accessible only to library staff.
  • Contact form messages: Name, email, and message content when you contact us.

How We Use Your Information

We use your information solely to administer library services, communicate membership status, enforce borrowing policies, generate anonymised statistical reports, and improve our digital services. We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties except as required by Sri Lankan law.

Your Rights

You have the right to access, correct, or request deletion of your personal data. To make a request, contact the library at privacy@batticaloalibrary.lk or visit in person with photo ID. We will respond within 30 days.

Cookies & Local Storage

This website uses a single HTTP-only session cookie for authentication and browser localStorage to remember your language preference and theme choice. No advertising or tracking cookies are used.

Intellectual Freedom

The library is committed to protecting patron confidentiality. Borrowing records are considered confidential and will not be disclosed to any party — including law enforcement — without a valid court order.

Computer & Internet Use Policy

The library provides free public computer and Wi-Fi access to all members and visitors. By using library technology resources, you agree to the following terms:

  • Sessions are limited to 60 minutes per day when others are waiting.
  • Priority access is given to members working on academic or research tasks.
  • Viewing, downloading, or sharing illegal, offensive, or harmful content is strictly prohibited.
  • Users may not attempt to bypass library content filters or engage in hacking activities.
  • Personal data must not be left on library computers — always log out of all accounts.
  • Commercial use (running a business from library computers) is not permitted without prior approval.
  • Printing is available at LKR 5 per black-and-white page, LKR 15 per colour page.

Code of Conduct

All library users are expected to treat staff, other patrons, and library property with respect. The following behaviours are not permitted:

  • Disruptive noise — please use quiet voices in reading areas.
  • Harassment, discrimination, or threatening behaviour toward any person.
  • Vandalism or damage to library property or materials.
  • Eating or drinking near computer terminals or in reading rooms (designated café area excepted).
  • Smoking or vaping anywhere on library premises.
  • Soliciting, hawking, or canvassing without written permission from the library director.

Violations may result in removal from the premises and, for serious or repeated offences, suspension or cancellation of membership. Decisions may be appealed to the Head Librarian within 14 days.

Accessibility Statement (WCAG 2.1)

Batticaloa Public Library is committed to making its digital platform accessible to all users, including people with disabilities. We aim to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA.

Measures Taken

  • Semantic HTML5 elements and ARIA landmarks throughout the site.
  • All images include descriptive alt text.
  • Colour contrast ratios meet AA requirements for all text elements.
  • All interactive elements are reachable by keyboard navigation.
  • Focus indicators are clearly visible.
  • Dark/light theme options to reduce eye strain.
  • Support for browser zoom up to 200% without horizontal scrolling.
  • Multilingual support in English, Tamil (தமிழ்), and Sinhala (සිංහල).

Physical Accessibility

The main library building features ramp access, accessible restrooms, and designated seating for patrons with disabilities. Large-print books and screen-reader compatible terminals are available on request.

Feedback

If you experience any accessibility barriers, please contact us at access@batticaloalibrary.lk or call 065-2222484 / 077-6718944. We aim to resolve accessibility concerns within 5 business days.

Koha Integrated Library System (ILS) Data Policy

Batticaloa Public Library uses the Koha open-source ILS, shared with the Eastern University Library and partner institutions. The following applies to data within the shared Koha system:

  • Bibliographic data is shared across all partner institutions for catalog discovery.
  • Patron data is institution-specific and not shared across partner libraries.
  • The Koha OPAC (Online Public Access Catalog) is publicly accessible for catalog searches.
  • Loan records within Koha are accessible only to authorised library staff.
  • Koha system data is backed up daily and retained according to each institution's data retention policy.

Questions about the Koha system may be directed to systems@batticaloalibrary.lk.
