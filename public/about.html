About the Library

Serving the Batticaloa Municipal Council area since 1972, providing books and information services to the people of the Eastern Province.

Our Story

A Heritage of Learning

Established in 1972, the Batticaloa Public Library has grown from a modest reading room into the main public library of the Eastern Province. Through decades of social change and rebuilding, the library has remained a place of learning for readers, researchers and students across the region.

Today, in partnership with the Municipal Council, we are completing a digital transformation that brings together multiple library systems under one platform, making the collection accessible to all members online.

Our Journey

Historical Milestones

1972

Foundation

The Batticaloa Public Library was established, serving Tamil, Sinhala and Muslim communities of Batticaloa. 3,000 founding volumes formed the multilingual collection, making it a cornerstone of learning in the Eastern Province.

1995

Expansion

New main branch opened on Bar Road with dedicated reading rooms, children's section and reference collection.

2010

Digital Catalog Launch

Library catalog fully digitized, enabling members to search the collection online.

2018

Digital Services Expansion

Free Wi-Fi, computer lab and Maker Space launched.

2026

Unified Digital Library Now

Launch of the integrated digital library platform, bringing together all branch catalogs for members across the Eastern Province.
