About the Library

Serving the Eastern Province since 1855 — preserving heritage, empowering communities.

Our Story

A Heritage of Learning

Established in 1855, the Batticaloa Public Library has grown from a modest reading room into a comprehensive knowledge institution. Through decades of social change and rebuilding, the library has remained a steadfast home for readers, researchers, students and dreamers across the Eastern Province.

Today, in partnership with the Eastern University and the Municipal Council, we are completing a digital transformation that brings together multiple library systems under one platform — making knowledge truly accessible to all.

Our Journey

Historical Milestones

1855

Foundation

The Batticaloa Public Library was established, becoming one of the earliest public knowledge institutions in the Eastern Province, serving the multilingual community of Batticaloa.

1972

Modernisation

Major institutional modernization and expansion — the library was re-inaugurated with 3,000 new volumes donated by the community, expanding its multilingual collections.

1995

Expansion

New main branch opened on Bar Road with dedicated reading rooms, children's section and reference collection.

2010

Koha Migration

Library catalog fully digitized using the Koha Integrated Library System — enabling OPAC access from anywhere.

2018

Digital Services & Eastern University Integration

Free Wi-Fi, computer lab and Maker Space launched. Eastern University catalog integration begins at Vantharumoolai branch.

2026

Unified Digital Library Now

Launch of the integrated digital library platform connecting Koha, Eastern University, and Open Library — serving every citizen of the Eastern Province.
