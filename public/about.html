About the Library

Serving the Eastern Province since 1972 — preserving heritage, empowering communities.

Our Story

A Heritage of Learning

Established in 1972, the Batticaloa Public Library has grown from a modest reading room into a comprehensive knowledge institution. Through decades of social change and rebuilding, the library has remained a steadfast home for readers, researchers, students and dreamers across the Eastern Province.

Today, in partnership with the Eastern University and the Municipal Council, we are completing a digital transformation that brings together multiple library systems under one platform — making knowledge truly accessible to all.

Vision & Mission

Our Purpose

🌟

Vision

To be a modern, digitally connected, community-centered knowledge hub that promotes lifelong learning, research, innovation and cultural preservation.

🎯

Mission

To provide free and equitable access to information, learning resources and cultural heritage for every member of the Batticaloa community.

💎

Values

Inclusivity, integrity, accessibility, intellectual freedom, community service, and continuous innovation in library services.

Our Journey

Historical Milestones

1972

Foundation

The Batticaloa Public Library was inaugurated in a single hall with 3,000 volumes donated by the community.

1995

Expansion

New main branch opened on Bar Road with dedicated reading rooms, children's section and reference collection.

2010

Koha Migration

Library catalog fully digitized using the Koha Integrated Library System — enabling OPAC access from anywhere.

2018

Digital Services & Eastern University Integration

Free Wi-Fi, computer lab and Maker Space launched. Eastern University catalog integration begins at Vantharumoolai branch.

2026

Unified Digital Library Now

Launch of the integrated digital library platform connecting Koha, Eastern University, and Open Library — serving every citizen of the Eastern Province.
